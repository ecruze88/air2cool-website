Your heating and cooling system is one of the most expensive pieces of equipment in your home, and one of the most neglected. An HVAC maintenance plan is essentially insurance against the worst-case scenario: your furnace dying in February or your AC failing on the hottest day of the year. But is it actually worth the cost? We will break down exactly what a good plan covers, how it compares to the cost of emergency repairs, and what Air2Cool's maintenance plan includes for North NJ homeowners.

## What Does an HVAC Maintenance Plan Include?

A solid HVAC maintenance plan typically covers two visits per year, one in fall before heating season, one in spring before cooling season. At each visit, a licensed technician runs a comprehensive inspection and tune-up of your system. Here is what that looks like at Air2Cool:

### Fall Heating Tune-Up

- Inspect and clean heat exchanger
- Check flue and venting for blockages
- Test ignition system and safety controls
- Inspect and clean burners
- Check gas pressure and connections
- Lubricate blower motor bearings
- Test thermostat calibration
- Measure temperature rise across heat exchanger
- Replace air filter (standard 1-inch)
- Carbon monoxide check

### Spring Cooling Tune-Up

- Check refrigerant charge and inspect for leaks
- Clean condenser coils
- Inspect and straighten condenser fins
- Test capacitors and contactors
- Check electrical connections and wiring
- Measure amperage on compressor and fan motors
- Clear condensate drain line
- Inspect evaporator coil
- Test thermostat and system controls
- Replace air filter (standard 1-inch)

This is a thorough inspection, not a 20-minute walkthrough. A proper tune-up catches small issues (a worn capacitor, a cracked heat exchanger, a clogged drain line) before they turn into emergency repairs or system failures. ## Annual vs. Bi-Annual Visits: What's the Difference?

Some companies offer maintenance plans with just one visit per year. For most New Jersey homes, where you run the heat hard from November through March and the AC hard from June through September, one visit per year is not enough.

**One visit per year** makes sense if you have a newer system (under 5 years old) with no history of issues, or if your home has separate heating and cooling systems on different schedules.

**Two visits per year** is the right call for most NJ homeowners. Heating and cooling are two separate systems with different components that need to be checked seasonally. A spring AC tune-up and a fall heating tune-up keep both systems dialed in year-round.

## Maintenance Plan Cost vs. Emergency Repair Cost

Here is the comparison that makes the math obvious: planned maintenance helps prevent the most common emergency scenarios, including summer AC failures, clogged drains, and major component breakdowns. The real value is not just the money, it is not losing heat in January or AC during a July heat wave.
