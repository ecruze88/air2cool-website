## Step 1: Check the Basics Before You Call

Before picking up the phone, run through a quick checklist. These are the most common "emergencies" that turn out to be simple fixes:

- **Check your thermostat.** Make sure it is set to "Cool" and the target temperature is below the current room temperature. A dead battery or a bumped setting is a surprisingly common culprit.
- **Check the air filter.** A clogged filter can cause the system to freeze up and stop blowing cold air. If it is gray and matted, replace it and give the system 30 minutes to reset.
- **Check your circuit breaker.** AC units draw a lot of power. A tripped breaker is common during peak summer heat. Reset it once. If it trips again, stop and call a technician.
- **Check the outdoor unit.** Is it running? Is there ice on the refrigerant lines? Ice on the lines usually means a refrigerant issue or a badly clogged filter, both require a licensed HVAC tech.

## Common Causes of AC Breakdowns in Summer

After more than 25 years of HVAC service in North Jersey, our techs have seen every type of AC failure. The most common reasons systems fail during peak heat:

- **Failed capacitor.** Capacitors start the compressor and fan motors. They wear out over time and often fail when the unit works hardest. Replacement is typically fast and affordable.
- **Low refrigerant.** If your system is low on refrigerant, it cannot absorb heat effectively. This usually means a leak somewhere in the system. Adding refrigerant without finding the leak is only a temporary fix.
- **Dirty condenser coils.** The outdoor unit's coils reject heat to the outside air. When they get coated with dirt and debris, efficiency drops and the system can overheat.
- **Frozen evaporator coil.** Restricted airflow or low refrigerant can cause the indoor coil to freeze solid. The unit will blow warm air (or no air) until the ice melts and the root cause is fixed.
- **Failed compressor.** The compressor is the heart of your AC. When it fails, the system stops cooling entirely. Compressor replacement is one of the larger repairs. Depending on the age of your system, replacement may make more sense.
