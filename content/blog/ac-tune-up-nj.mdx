There's a reason we recommend scheduling before Memorial Day. Once temperatures climb and everyone's AC kicks on for the first time, the phones light up. Technicians get booked out days in advance and emergency calls take priority. ## What a Professional AC Tune-Up Includes

Not all tune-ups are created equal. A real preventative maintenance visit should cover the following:

- **Refrigerant level check:** Low refrigerant is one of the top causes of poor cooling performance. We check pressures and look for signs of leaks.
- **Evaporator and condenser coil cleaning:** Dirty coils force the system to work harder, driving up your electric bill and shortening equipment life.
- **Electrical connections and capacitor test:** Loose connections and a weakening capacitor are leading causes of summer breakdowns, and both are easy to catch during a tune-up.
- **Thermostat calibration:** If your thermostat is reading off by a few degrees, your system runs longer than necessary.
- **Condensate drain flush:** A clogged drain line causes water damage and can trigger a system shutoff. We clear it as standard.
- **Blower inspection:** A dirty or unbalanced blower reduces airflow throughout your home, which often goes unnoticed until comfort complaints start coming in.

At the end of the visit, we tell you exactly what we found, including anything that should be addressed before summer. No upsells, just an honest report.

## Signs Your AC Needs More Than Just a Tune-Up

Sometimes a tune-up reveals a bigger issue. Here are warning signs that your system may need actual repair:

- **Warm air from the vents** even with the system running — often low refrigerant or a failing compressor.
- **Ice on the refrigerant lines or indoor unit** — a frozen evaporator coil that needs investigation.
- **The system won't start or keeps tripping the breaker** — could be a failing capacitor or compressor drawing too much current.
- **Unusual sounds** — grinding, banging, or squealing all point to mechanical issues that a tune-up alone won't fix.
