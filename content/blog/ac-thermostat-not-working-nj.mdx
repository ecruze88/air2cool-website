When the thermostat stops talking to the AC, the house heats up fast. In Morris County homes, we often see thermostat issues during the first hot week of the season. Here is how to troubleshoot safely.

## Common thermostat problems

- Dead batteries or loose battery contacts
- Thermostat set to Heat or Fan instead of Cool
- Incorrect wiring or loose wire terminals
- Tripped breaker for the air handler
- Old thermostat that cannot handle a newer system

## Quick checks you can do

- Replace the batteries even if the screen is on
- Confirm the mode is **Cool** and the set temperature is lower than room temp
- Check the breaker labeled air handler or furnace
- Ensure the thermostat is not in direct sunlight or near a vent

## When to call Air2Cool

- The thermostat is blank after new batteries
- Cooling still does not start after a reset
- You smell burning or see sparks at the thermostat
- Your system short cycles or runs nonstop
